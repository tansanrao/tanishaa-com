The exhibition system needed to coordinate room labels, large-format interventions, and printed matter without overloading the space. A small set of graphic moves carried most of the identity so the work on display remained the focus.

Scale testing was central to the process. Decisions that felt subtle on paper became dominant in the room, so the visual language was pared back and made more structural. Type, line, and block color became tools for orientation rather than decoration.

The finished toolkit supports clear navigation and gives the exhibition a single voice across temporary and permanent surfaces.
