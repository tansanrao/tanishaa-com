This work translated a large image archive into time-based sequences that could live across screens, event backdrops, and social cut-downs. The design challenge was to preserve the grain and emotional density of the source material while introducing a clear new rhythm.

Experiments focused on crop logic, speed shifts, and how text could enter the frame without flattening the underlying material. Rather than animate everything, the final system used pauses, fades, and repeated anchor frames to build recognition.

The result is a motion toolkit that stays legible across formats and gives archival material a renewed presence without treating it as static reference.
