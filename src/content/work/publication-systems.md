The project began with a question of pacing: how could a small-format publication feel expansive without relying on oversized gestures. The solution centered on a modular grid that let essays, marginal notes, and imagery move independently while remaining visibly related.

Early iterations tested how much hierarchy the system needed before it became prescriptive. Type scales were reduced, spacing did more of the organizing work, and repeated annotation motifs created continuity between essays written by different contributors.

The final publication system treats restraint as a design tool. It supports long reading sessions, lets imagery land with more weight, and leaves enough flexibility for future issues to evolve without abandoning the core identity.
